Our Robotic Products



Bluetooth Control

Bluetooth works by the simple principle of sending and receiving data in the form of radio waves. Every Bluetooth enabled device has a card-like attachment known as the Bluetooth adapter. It is this Bluetooth adapter that sends and receives data. We made a Bluetooth controlled robot car which was easy to operate using a mobile application. It’s simple in making and easy to understand. Its the kind of basics of robotics that every student above certain age must know.

Obstacle Avoiding

Obstacle avoiding is a concept used in the tesla self driving car which detects any human, vehicle or any obstacle in its proximity and changes the path of the vehicle to avoid collision. This concept is one of the defining pillars of the self driving car.

Drone

A four propeller drone with the idea of Aerial security, this a Four propeller drone with brushless dc motors.Range of this drone is 50 feet, with a FSI6 transmitter, made on Arduino. Drone serves many purposes from security, to delivery of products, without the use of manual pilot. Drone also has a lot of potential in the defense and military sector, We have a team working on the R&D of this team , to navigate the further possibilities in the world of aviation.

Why Robotics should be introduced in school.

In today’s technology-driven world, it’s important now more than ever to prepare students for the future. Teaching robotics to young students throughout their schooling can increase their ability to be creative and innovative thinkers and more productive members of society. Many governments have already recognized the importance of robotics in the classroom and have begun to create programs and laws that would incorporate it into their public education system. By teaching our students the basics of robotics, we can open a whole new world to them and exciting opportunities that they wouldn’t have access to otherwise.
